### What we do

**Core Mission**
Practi.City serves as a collaborative, open-contribution community, knowledge hub, and code repository dedicated to promoting and advancing experiential learning and real-scale simulation as the premier method for acquiring professional skills for the real world.

**Key Goals**
1. **Curates an Open-Source Knowledge Base:** It catalogs and deeply describes open-source experiential learning simulation tools (such as JaamSim and DWSim) and experiential learning methodologies (such as Practice Firms, real-scale simulations or advanced role-plays).
2. **Shares Community Stories:** It blogs about and highlights the organizations, initiatives, and people that actively use these tools and methodologies in the real world.
3. **Develops Open-Source Code:** It publishes, shares, and maintains code that extends existing simulation software (like JaamSim or DWSIM) and builds interfaces that connect these various solutions together.
4. **Integrates AI:** It promotes new AI practices and explores how artificial intelligence expands the possibilities of experiential learning and simulation.
5. **Establishes a Universal Experiential Simulation Manifesto:** It provides a foundational set of rules and principles that allow users to seamlessly combine their respective experiential learning paths and share them collaboratively.

### Important Disclaimer
Though we share certain common educational values, our community has no connection to the "Practicity" (or https://practicity.app) educational project aimed at teaching children life skills like finance and nutrition. While both share a name derived from "practicality," practi.city is a volunteer community aiming at promoting experiential and simulation based learning.
