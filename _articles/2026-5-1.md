Abstract: >-
  **Abstract: Threading the Supply Chain – A Material-First Approach to
  Sustainable Design Education**


  As experiential learning practitioners, we constantly seek ways to move students from abstract conceptualization to grounded, systems-level thinking. This video highlights a transformative pedagogical shift in a Knitwear Design course, where the traditional design process is fundamentally flipped to foster sustainability and deeper material connection. 


  Typically, design students begin with a visual prompt or story and select materials—often environmentally problematic synthetics—to execute their vision. Recognizing that "designers are problem solvers, but can also be problem makers," the instructor redesigned the curriculum to be *material-driven*. By introducing students to locally sourced wool, the learning experience becomes highly tactile and deeply rooted in the local agricultural ecosystem.  


  **Key Experiential Elements:**

  *   **Tactile Investigation:** Students physically interact with raw materials, learning to feel the difference in fiber structures (loft, felt, downiness) and testing the wool's tensile strength. They learn that a break in the fleece directly correlates to a sheep's past health or difficult pregnancy—connecting biological realities to design constraints.


  *   **Systems Thinking & Storytelling:** The curriculum transforms the supply chain into a narrative journey. Students trace the fiber from the specific animal, through the milling and spinning process, all the way to their own hands at the "tail end of manufacturing." 

  *   **Empathy and Stakeholder Engagement:** By getting to know the local farmers, the processors, and even the sheep themselves, students re-evaluate how material origins inform their final design choices.


  For educators and practitioners, this case study serves as a powerful model of how rooting a curriculum in local, tangible materials can transform students from passive consumers of synthetic goods into mindful designers and active participants in a sustainable supply chain.
