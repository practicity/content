They say professional relationships need regular maintenance. Recently, I caught up with Stefano, an old friend and former colleague from our ERP project days in Lyon.

We met at his new office: the CRA (Autism Resource Center) at Vinatier Hospital. It wasn't just any day; it was World Autism Awareness Day. A coincidence? Maybe, but it felt like a professional wake-up call.
Once we finished the small talk, I decided to pick his brain about training for neurodivergent adults. I had a hunch that for PractiCity, understanding how autistic people perceive the world could be a goldmine. If they see the world differently, their approach to experiential learning must be unique—and incredibly valuable.
Stefano pointed me toward Paul, the librarian and "keeper of knowledge." 

Meeting Paul a week later was eye-opening. The Reality Gap: while France is still struggling with entry-level integration, North America is already focusing on high-level career development.

The Scale: A quick search on the internet (WHO/OMS) confirms a staggering fact: neurodivergent conditions affect up to 20% of the population. Looking back at my career, I realized I’ve likely missed the mark many times. I could have adopted a much better managerial approach if I had been more aware.

This is where Experiential Learning changes the game. Simulation isn't just a tool for neurodivergent learners to test their skills in a "safe harbor." It can also prepare everyone else—managers and coworkers alike—to better understand and face the daily challenges of neurodiversity. It’s about building work relationships that are truly mutually beneficial.
I’m currently digging through the "juicy" info Paul shared to prepare a full article before the summer. 
A huge thanks to Stefano and Paul for this first deep dive.
