As designers, we aim to create a "holding space" where subtle signals and quiet voices can emerge. Intrusion "crowds out" these signals. Whether it’s the expert jumping in or the senior leader steering the agenda, these moves create an invisible boundary that prevents the group from tackling the "real," unspoken challenges.

These online seminar held by Etienne & Beverly Wenger-Trayner aim at sharing new theoretical thinking, practical tools including a new set of intrusion archetype cards currently in development, and — above all — the collective intelligence of a room full of practitioners who have been wrestling with these dynamics in their own work.
